Satvik Tajane
Robotics Engineer

Hi, I'm Satvik Tajane — a Master's student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Northeastern University, specializing in Hardware and Software for Machine Intelligence. My work centers on robotics perception, planning, and control, with particular interest in robotic manipulation and unmanned autonomous vehicles (UAVs). I completed my undergraduate degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering at MIT World Peace University, Pune.

My interest lies in teaching robots how to transform raw sensory information into meaningful understanding and purposeful actions.

I first encountered robotics as an optional subject during my undergraduate studies at MIT WPU — and it was a turning point. That initial exposure fascinated me deeply, and from that moment on, I've been building my career around it. Robotics isn't just my profession — it's my way of making the world an easier place to live.

Ro-Brew-To: Autonomous Coffee-Making Robot

Expected July 2026
ROS 2 Jazzy Gazebo Harmonic MoveIt 2

An autonomous pick-and-place pipeline for a UR3e 6-DOF manipulator with Robotiq 2F-85 gripper, simulating the full operation of a pod espresso machine — from detection to brew completion.

  • Designing and simulating a complete autonomous manipulation pipeline integrating perception, motion planning, and task orchestration on ROS 2 Jazzy
  • Built a custom Gazebo Harmonic world with articulated coffee machine model and overhead RGBD camera sensor
  • Implementing marker-based 6D pose estimation using OpenCV ArUco detection, projecting pixel coordinates to world frame via camera intrinsics and TF2 transforms
  • Developing pick-and-place sequences using MoveIt 2 with Cartesian arc trajectories for lever actuation and constrained planning for pod insertion

Degradation-Aware Particle Filter for Robot Localization

January 2026 – April 2026
Gazebo LiDAR Particle Filter

A custom particle filter with dynamic sensor weighting that maintains robust localization even under severe sensor degradation, outperforming the Nav2 AMCL baseline.

  • Built a custom Gazebo simulation environment integrating LiDAR, camera, and IMU sensor plugins for a ground robot with controlled multi-sensor degradation experiments
  • Designed a degradation-aware particle filter with sensor fallback, introducing 4 levels of synthetic degradation to LiDAR and camera data with a confidence monitor for dynamic weighting
  • Outperformed Nav2 AMCL baseline by 1 m in Absolute Trajectory Error (ATE RMSE), demonstrating robust localization under sensor failure conditions
